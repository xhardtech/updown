Questions people also search

What is a cobot and how is it different from a robot?

A cobot (collaborative robot) is designed to work safely alongside humans without safety cages. Traditional industrial robots operate behind barriers. Cobots are smaller, lighter, easier to program, and can be redeployed to different tasks. Leading brands: Universal Robots, Fanuc, ABB, KUKA.

How much does a cobot cost in Vietnam?

A Universal Robots UR5e costs $25,000-35,000. A UR10e costs $35,000-50,000. With grippers, sensors, and integration, total deployment cost is $40,000-80,000. Payback period: 12-18 months in a typical Vietnamese factory running 2-3 shifts.

What is the ROI of cobots in manufacturing?

Typical ROI: 12-18 months payback. A cobot running 3 shifts replaces 2-3 workers ($300/month each = $7,200-10,800/year). At $40,000 total cost, it pays for itself in ~18 months. Plus: no sick days, consistent quality, 24/7 operation.

Are cobots safe to work alongside humans?

Yes — cobots are certified to ISO/TS 15066 for collaborative operation. They have force-limiting sensors that stop on contact, speed monitoring, and hand-guiding modes. No safety cages required. This is a fundamental design requirement, not an afterthought.

What tasks can cobots do in factories?

Common cobot tasks: pick-and-place, machine tending, quality inspection, welding, screwdriving, palletizing, polishing, and assembly. Cobots excel at repetitive, precision tasks. They're not suited for heavy-lifting (>16kg) or tasks requiring full-body mobility.

Universal Robots vs Fanuc vs ABB — which is best?

Universal Robots: largest ecosystem (1,100+ UR+ partners), easiest to program, best for SMEs. Fanuc: highest precision, best for high-speed manufacturing. ABB: strongest in heavy industry, best payload range. In Vietnam, UR dominates SME adoption; Fanuc leads in FDI factories.
