Questions people also search

Why is GPT better than open-source models?

GPT-4.5 leads on the hardest reasoning, coding, and multimodal tasks. OpenAI invests $1B+ per training run with proprietary RLHF data from millions of users. Open-source models close the gap on average benchmarks but consistently trail on frontier capabilities.

What is Claude and how does it compare to GPT?

Claude (by Anthropic) is a closed-source LLM family. Claude Opus 4 leads on long-context tasks, coding, and safety. It competes directly with GPT-4.5 and Gemini Ultra. Both are closed-source, highlighting that the top tier of AI remains proprietary.

How much does it cost to train a frontier LLM?

Training a frontier model costs $100M-$1B+ (compute alone). GPT-4 reportedly cost $100M+. Google Gemini Ultra likely exceeded that. Open-source efforts rely on donated compute, government grants, or distillation from closed models — a structural disadvantage.

What is RLHF and why does it matter?

RLHF (Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback) uses human preferences to align AI outputs. OpenAI and Anthropic have millions of real user interactions for RLHF. Open-source projects have far less human feedback data — this "data moat" is hard to replicate.

Are closed-source LLMs safer than open-source?

Closed-source providers invest heavily in safety: red-teaming, content filtering, jailbreak prevention. Open-source models can be modified to remove safety guardrails. For enterprise compliance and liability, closed-source offers contractual guarantees that open-source cannot.

Will open-source ever catch up to GPT/Claude?

Open-source has closed the gap on benchmarks like MMLU and HumanEval. But frontier labs keep moving the goal post — when open-source reaches GPT-4 level, GPT-5 is already out. The question isn't whether open-source "catches up" but whether the frontier keeps advancing faster.
