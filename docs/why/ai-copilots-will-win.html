Questions people also search

What is a human-in-the-loop AI system?

Human-in-the-loop (HITL) AI keeps humans in the decision chain — the AI suggests, the human approves. This model powers tools like GitHub Copilot, ChatGPT with confirmation, and enterprise AI assistants. It's the approach regulators and enterprise leaders prefer for accountability.

Why do enterprises prefer AI copilots over autonomous agents?

78% of enterprise executives (Deloitte 2025) won't deploy AI without human approval for critical decisions. Reasons: legal liability, regulatory compliance (EU AI Act, Vietnam AI Law), error cost in finance/healthcare, and the need for explainable decisions in audits.

Is GitHub Copilot an AI agent or a copilot?

GitHub Copilot is primarily a copilot — it suggests code completions and the developer decides whether to accept. However, newer features like "Copilot Workspace" blur the line by automating multi-step tasks. The core product remains human-in-the-loop.

What regulations require human oversight of AI?

Key regulations: EU AI Act (2024) requires human oversight for high-risk AI. Vietnam's AI Law (March 2026) mandates human approval for AI affecting safety/rights. US Executive Order 14110 emphasizes responsible AI deployment. These laws favor the copilot model over full autonomy.

Can AI copilots be as fast as autonomous agents?

Copilots are inherently slower because they wait for human approval. However, the speed difference matters less than the accuracy difference. A copilot that's right 99% of the time produces better outcomes than an agent that's right 95% — especially when errors are costly.

What happens when an AI agent makes a mistake?

With autonomous agents, mistakes propagate before detection — an agent can send wrong emails, deploy broken code, or make bad financial decisions before anyone notices. With copilots, humans catch errors before they happen. The liability question remains unsettled in most legal systems.
