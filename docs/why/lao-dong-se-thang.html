Why you might be right

💰 Vietnamese wages are still far cheaper than robots

$300/month = $3,600/year. A humanoid robot currently costs $50,000-150,000. Even if prices drop to $20,000, it still takes 5-6 years to break even. And robots need maintenance, spare parts, and operating engineers — the hidden costs are enormous.

💪 Flexibility — something robots don't have

Vietnamese workers switch between production tasks within a single day. They handle defective products, unexpected situations, and last-minute customer changes. Robots can only do what they're programmed for. Vietnamese factories produce diverse products — robots simply don't fit.

🔧 The infrastructure isn't ready

Most Vietnamese factories still lack: stable 24/7 power, 5G connectivity in industrial zones, robot maintenance teams, and automated management systems. Deploying 100 robots requires a full factory overhaul — costing an additional $5-10 million.

🗺 Politics — a barrier you can't ignore

Replacing millions of workers means social instability. The Vietnamese government prioritizes stability above all else. No administration will allow mass layoffs to make way for robots. This is a political barrier, not a technical one.

📈 What does history tell us?

"Robots will replace everything" — people have been saying this since the 1960s. But 60 years later, workers are still here. Every time robots were "about to take over," reality turned out far more complex than the demos. Don't trust the hype — trust reality.

🧠 Humans have what robots never will

Creativity. Judgment. Communication. Solving problems never seen before. In real-world manufacturing, 30-40% of tasks demand adaptability that robots can't match — and likely won't for the next decade.

🌏 FDI still chooses Vietnam for its workers

Samsung, Intel, Foxconn, LG — they all came to Vietnam BECAUSE of affordable, hardworking labor. If they only needed robots, they'd have stayed in South Korea or Japan. The reality: they need both — and human workers remain the decisive factor.

Counter-evidence you've already considered

📈 "Robot prices are dropping fast"

True, prices are falling. But the "robot price" is only 30% of total deployment cost. Factory retrofitting, engineer training, maintenance, spare parts, downtime — the real total cost is far higher than the catalog price.

👨‍🦳 "Vietnam's population is aging, labor will be scarce"

The aging trend is real — but Vietnam will still have a younger population than most of Asia until 2035. And when labor becomes scarce, wages rise, meaning better income for workers. That's a GOOD thing, not a problem robots need to solve.

🌐 "FDI will force the change"

FDI came to Vietnam because of cheap labor. If robots were cheaper, they'd build robot factories in their home countries — no need to come to Vietnam at all. Vietnamese labor is the reason FDI exists here.
