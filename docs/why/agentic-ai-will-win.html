Questions people also search

What is agentic AI vs copilot AI?

Agentic AI operates autonomously — it decides, acts, and iterates without waiting for human approval at each step. AI copilots (like GitHub Copilot) suggest actions but require a human to review and approve. The key difference: agents act independently, copilots assist humans.

Is agentic AI safe for enterprise use?

As of 2026, agent error rates have dropped below 5% on standard benchmarks. However, most enterprises still require human oversight for high-risk decisions. The safety gap is closing rapidly — Gartner predicts agent reliability will match human accuracy by 2028 for most business processes.

What are examples of agentic AI tools?

Leading agentic AI tools include Claude Code (autonomous coding), Devin (AI software engineer), Cursor Agent Mode, AutoGPT, and enterprise solutions from Salesforce (Agentforce), Microsoft (Copilot Studio agents), and Google (Vertex AI agents). These tools complete multi-step tasks without human intervention.

Will AI agents replace software developers?

AI agents are augmenting developers, not replacing them. They handle repetitive tasks (code review, testing, refactoring) 10-50x faster. Developers shift to architecture, product thinking, and overseeing agent outputs. The role changes, but demand for human judgment remains.

What does Vietnam's AI Law say about autonomous AI?

Vietnam's AI Law (effective March 1, 2026) requires human oversight for "high-risk" AI applications affecting health, safety, or rights. Most routine business processes aren't classified as high-risk. The law includes sandbox provisions for testing autonomous systems.

Agentic AI vs RPA — what's the difference?

RPA (Robotic Process Automation) follows pre-programmed rules on structured data. Agentic AI understands context, handles unstructured data, makes decisions, and adapts to new situations. RPA automates known workflows; agentic AI can handle novel tasks it's never seen before.
