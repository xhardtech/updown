The case for Japanese cars staying on top

1. Decades of proven reliability

A Toyota Vios can run 300,000km and still be solid. A Honda City hits 200,000km without major repairs. VinFast has only been around since 2019 — nobody knows what a VinFast looks like after 10 years.

2. Resale value — Japanese cars hold their worth

A Toyota Vios retains 75-80% of its value after 3 years. A VinFast VF8 loses 40-50% after just 1 year. When you sell, Japanese car owners get significantly more back — that's real money.

3. Extensive dealership and service network

Toyota has 70+ dealerships across the country, reaching even remote provinces. Honda is similar. VinFast is still building out — if you live in a rural area, where do you get your car serviced?

4. No battery or charging worries

Replacing a VinFast battery: 200-400 million VND. Charging in an apartment building? Very difficult. Long road trips? Requires careful planning. Gas cars: fill up in 5 minutes, gas stations everywhere, zero stress.

5. Hybrids — the perfect middle ground

6. Market reality — Japanese cars still lead

The Toyota Vios has been Vietnam's best-selling car for years running. Japanese car market share remains above 50%. The numbers don't lie — Vietnamese buyers are still choosing Japanese cars with their real money.

Counter-evidence you've already considered

The global EV trend

Yes, EVs are the future. But "the future" could be 10-20 years away. Right now, Vietnam's infrastructure isn't ready. Buying a car is a decision for today, not for 2035.

Lower operating costs

Electricity is cheaper than gas, true. But when you factor in depreciation and battery replacement costs, the total cost of ownership isn't necessarily lower. You need to do the full math, not just look at the electricity bill.

Government incentives

Registration tax exemption only lasts through 2027. After that? It'll be the same as any other car. A temporary incentive shouldn't be the main reason to buy a car you'll drive for 10 years.

Questions people also search

Toyota Vios vs VinFast VF5 — which should I buy?

Toyota Vios: proven reliability (300,000km+), 75-80% resale value, nationwide service. VinFast VF5: cheaper to run (electricity vs gas), modern features, but unproven long-term and lower resale value. If you prioritize reliability and resale: Vios. If you prioritize running costs and tech: VF5.

Do Japanese cars hold their value in Vietnam?

Yes — Japanese cars have the best resale value in Vietnam. Toyota Vios: 75-80% after 3 years. Honda City: 70-75%. Toyota Fortuner: 80-85%. Compare to VinFast VF8: 50-60% after 1 year. For buyers who change cars every 3-5 years, resale value is a major financial factor.

Are hybrid cars better than EVs in Vietnam?

For Vietnam in 2026: arguably yes. Toyota Yaris Cross Hybrid cuts fuel 50% without needing charging infrastructure. Honda City e:HEV offers 3.8L/100km. Hybrids work everywhere — no charging stations needed, no range anxiety, no battery replacement costs. Best of both worlds.

Why is Toyota still the #1 selling brand in Vietnam?

Trust built over 30+ years. The Vios has been Vietnam's best-selling car for years. Reasons: proven reliability, 70+ dealerships nationwide (including rural areas), affordable maintenance, strong resale value, and word-of-mouth from millions of satisfied owners.

What are the hidden costs of owning an EV in Vietnam?

Battery replacement: 200-400 million VND (after warranty). Home charging installation: 5-15 million VND. Apartment charging: often impossible. Depreciation: 40-50% in year 1 for VinFast. Insurance: 10-15% higher than gas cars. Long trips: planning around charger locations.

Honda City vs Toyota Vios — which is better?

Toyota Vios: better resale value, more trunk space, smoother ride. Honda City: better engine performance, sportier driving, more standard features. Both are equally reliable. Price is similar (~550-650 million VND). In Vietnam, Vios outsells City due to brand trust and resale.
