Questions people also search

What is an AMR (Autonomous Mobile Robot)?

An AMR is a self-navigating robot that moves materials through factories and warehouses without fixed tracks or rails. Unlike AGVs (Automated Guided Vehicles), AMRs use LIDAR, cameras, and AI to map their environment and navigate around obstacles dynamically. Amazon uses 750,000+ AMRs.

AMR vs AGV — what's the difference?

AGVs follow fixed paths (magnetic strips, wires). AMRs navigate freely using AI and sensors. AGVs are cheaper but inflexible. AMRs cost more but can adapt to layout changes instantly. For modern factories with changing workflows, AMRs are the clear winner.

What is AI Vision quality inspection?

AI Vision uses cameras + deep learning to detect product defects, measure dimensions, and verify assembly. Accuracy: 99.5% vs human inspectors at 95-97%. It works 24/7 without fatigue. Samsung and Intel already deploy AI Vision in their Vietnamese factories.

How much does an AMR cost?

Entry-level AMR: $5,000-15,000. AI Vision camera system: $500-2,000 per station. Total deployment for a small factory: $30,000-100,000. Compare: a cobot deployment costs $40,000-80,000 for a single station. AMR+AI gives broader coverage at lower cost.

Can Vietnamese companies build AMR and AI Vision?

Yes — FPT, Viettel, and several startups are developing AMR and AI Vision solutions tailored for Vietnamese factories. This means local support, Vietnamese-language interfaces, and prices 30-50% lower than imported solutions. Technology sovereignty is a real advantage.

AMR vs cobot vs humanoid — which should my factory buy?

AMR: best for transport and logistics (60-70% of factory work). Cobot: best for precision manipulation (welding, assembly). Humanoid: not production-ready yet. For most Vietnamese factories starting automation, AMR+AI Vision offers the lowest-risk, highest-ROI entry point.
